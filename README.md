# **📱 Design Sprint: From Storyboard to SwiftUI**

---

## **📝 Scenario**

You are part of a **senior iOS development team** at **Philbert.io**, a product-driven company undergoing a critical transition.

Your team has inherited **ElementQuiz**, an educational app originally built using **Storyboard**.

However, the company is modernizing its entire iOS ecosystem using **SwiftUI**.

This is not just a rebuild.

It is a **strategic shift**:

* From _imperative_ UI to _declarative_ UI
* From _isolated_ coding to _system_ thinking
* From _individual_ contribution to _technical_ _leadership_

At the same time, your team is responsible for **mentoring junior developers**, many of whom have never used SwiftUI.

---

## **🚨 The Challenge**

Your team must:

1. Redesign and rebuild ElementQuiz using SwiftUI
2. Create a system that supports junior developer learning
3. Balance product delivery with mentorship

This is not just about writing code.

It’s about **how senior developers think, design, and lead in an AI-driven world**.

---

# **🏃‍♀️🏃‍♂️ Design Thinking Sprint**

You will follow the **Design Thinking Process**:

**Empathy → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test**

---

# **💕 1. EMPATHY**

**Philbert.io** is a forward-thinking software company focused on rebuilding and modernizing digital products for a rapidly evolving, AI-driven world.

**Mission**

To reimagine, rebuild, and reignite software systems while empowering developers to grow into adaptable, high-impact engineers.

### **Client Needs**

* Rebuild ElementQuiz in SwiftUI
* Design systems that others can understand and extend
* Help junior developers successfully transition to SwiftUI

### **Pain Points**

* Limited SwiftUI experience across team
* Risk of slowing down development
* Inconsistent architecture and practices

---

# **🎯 2. DEFINE**

### **Core Problem**
The company must migrate ElementQuiz to SwiftUI while enabling developers to learn, collaborate, and maintain delivery speed.

### **Define question**
How might we rebuild ElementQuiz in SwiftUI in a way that is clean, scalable, and easy for others to understand?

### **Success Criteria**
- A functional SwiftUI version of ElementQuiz with clear structure and reusable components
- Evidence that junior developers can understand and learn from the codebase (e.g., documentation, clarity, onboarding support)

---

# **💡 3. IDEATE**

Move beyond problems into **solutions and systems**.

### **Your Focus**

You are not just designing an app—you are designing:

* A **codebase**
* A **learning experience**
* A **team workflow**

---

### **Teaching & Mentorship**

* How will junior developers learn SwiftUI through this project?
* What concepts must be taught explicitly?
* How can learning be embedded into development?

---

### **Deliverables**

* Teaching strategy (infographic, slides, guides, walkthroughs, examples)

---

# **👩‍💻👨‍💻 4. PROTOTYPE**

You will bring your ideas to life.

### **Your Prototype Must Include**

#### **A. SwiftUI Code as Teaching**

Your code should teach.

Include:

* Clear naming and structure
* Comments explaining key SwiftUI concepts

---

#### **B. Learning Artifacts**

Create at least **one teaching resource**:

* Beginner-friendly SwiftUI infographic
* Code walkthrough (video or slide deck)
* “Storyboard → SwiftUI” comparison visual
---

### **Goal**

A junior developer should be able to:

* Understand your code
* Learn from your structure
* Build on your work

---

# **📱 5. TEST**

Testing is not just about functionality.

It is about **clarity, usability, and teachability**.

---

### **Test With Two Perspectives**

#### **1. Product Perspective**

* Does the app work?
* Is the UI intuitive?

#### **2. Learning Perspective**

* Can a junior developer understand your code?
* Are concepts clearly explained?
---

### **Methods**

* Peer reviews
* Code walkthrough sessions
* Feedback from “junior developers” (classmates)

---

### **Deliverables**

* Feedback summary
* Key improvements made
* Reflection on:

  * Technical decisions
  * Teaching effectiveness

---

# **✅ Final Deliverables**

Your team will submit:

### **1. SwiftUI App Prototype**

* Functional core features
* Clean, scalable structure

### **2. Teaching Artifact**

* Infographic, guide, walkthrough, or onboarding resource

### **4. Reflection**

* What did you learn about:

  * SwiftUI?
  * System design?
  * Teaching others?
  * The evolving role of developers?

---

# **🌏 Why This Matters**

In an AI-driven world:

* Coding is no longer enough
* Implementation is increasingly automated

What matters is:

* **How you think**
* **How you design systems**
* **How you help others grow**

---

## **🤔 Final Thought**

You are not being evaluated as junior developers.

You are being evaluated as **future senior developers**:

* Can you navigate ambiguity?
* Can you design for others?
* Can you build and teach at the same time?
